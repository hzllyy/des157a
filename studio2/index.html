My grandma and I met in the year 2002.

;

She taught me how to fold dumplings. So now when I miss her, I fold many, many, many dumplings.

;

My grandma spent the colder part of the year in China, so she was away when she passed. When my mom went home last year, she found the following note in my grandma’s nightstand. It was a throwaway scribble on a napkin, a hastily written declaration that my grandma was mine.

;

When it’s Lunar New Year, I like to invite my friends over to wrap dumplings together.

;

My dad and I also met in the year 2002.

Growing up, when people asked me what my dad did for work, I would just say “computer.” As a budding Silicon Valley SWE in the early 2000s, my dad did what any smart man would do: put me in front of a computer and say, “this is the future, so start getting familiar.”

;

My dad wanted me to be a doctor, but he was happy when I showed interest in coding anyway. He tells me that building games is a waste of time, but then buys me books on building games.

;

When I look at Mr. Worm, I remember to be grateful to my dad.

;

My sister and I met in the year 3812 B.C.E.

;

My sister loved anime as a kid. I was a faithful servant, so I followed my sister in watching pirated anime off youtube, which eventually led us to learning how to draw, which eventually led to digital art. By the end of the 2020 quarantine, sister and I were both running art Instagrams with thousands of followers.

; ;

She encouraged me to participate in fan projects. Designing proved to be pretty fun.

; ;

Whenever I finish a project for my design classes, I show my sister before I turn it in.
